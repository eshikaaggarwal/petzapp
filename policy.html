    • Pets or Animals shouldn’t be handed over to personnel who may use the animals for unlawful activities.

  3. Guidelines for Rehoming Pets and Animals
    1. For Pet Finders
      • Get Pets only from breeders registered with the State Animal Welfare Board. Visit www.awbi.in .
      • You should be at least 18 years old to "Rehome a pet".
      • Get the help of a Veterinarian before Rehoming a pet.
      • Never be a pet parent without prior experience, or without the guidance of an expert.
      • Report to the respective authorities if you find animals are being mistreated by the breeders.
      • Never get pregnant animals.
      • Always ask for Microchip number and certificates from the breeder.
    2. For Pet Breeders
      • Puppies and Kittens should be at least 8 weeks old.
      • Indian/Local pet breeds cannot be rehomed through Petzapp.
      • Register with Animal welfare boards of the respective state. Visit www.awbi.in .
      • Maintain a record book with the particulars of breeders and suppliers of pet animals, trade transactions and their details. Records of the customers buying pet animals must also be kept.
      • It is mandatory to provide animals with a minimum standard for sanitation, ventilation, heating, cooling, humidity, enclosure requirements, nutrition and medical treatment.
      • The registration certificate issued by the Kennel Club of India /other kennel clubs, with details such as the breed of the animal, its standard, and other relevant information mentioned thereupon, shall be issued.
      • The breeder shall furnish to the buyer in writing, details of feeding, dates of vaccination and deworming of the pet and the name and address of the licensed veterinarian who has examined the animal.
      • All breeders shall screen prospective buyers to ascertain their potential to properly take care for the breed, especially if it is a large one; and to ascertain whether they shall be able to provide the requisite training, and attend to grooming, socializing, veterinary expenses, equipment needs, spatial needs, and exercise requirements.
      • The breeder shall keep track of all puppies produced and rehomed, whether pet or for a show, to track how the breeding program is working. The buyer of the pup/dog shall be liable to furnish information with regard to health problems, physical abnormalities or death of the animal on a yearly basis.
      • Individual medical records shall be maintained of all animals purchased/bought, raised, or otherwise received/acquired, held, kept, maintained, sold, donated or otherwise disposed off, including by death or euthanasia.
      • These records shall also specify all treatments and medication given and all procedures performed on the animals, and include reasons for or the condition requiring the treatment, medication or procedure. The results of the treatment, medication or procedure shall be included in this record.

      4. Users who violate the prohibited items policy and or the restricted items policy may be subject to the following actions.

  4. Important Information to All Pet Lovers

    5. For the general information of pet lovers planning to own a pet, we give below some precautionary and possible steps that may be taken in order to avoid being taken for a ride by some of the unscrupulous breeders.

    1. Do not make any financial commitments before you see the pet/animal physically.
    2. Take the help of a Veterinary Doctor.
    3. Ensure yourself that you get whatever is promised.
    4. Insist for a copy of the registration certificate, if the Breeder/seller claims so.
    5. Always insist and get a proper agreement executed and signed by both parties involved which again will help in case of any dispute.
    6. Never commit yourself just on the basis of an email / Facebook or a chat through any other social media.
